Christmas Hero

In this project a Christmas themed game was developed in the course Computer Game Design at KTH during six weeks.

In the game, Christmas is behind the , but the Christmas Spirit has disappeard due to the Cactus Boss. Konsolofon

In the course Music Acoustics at KTH, this instrument was developed together with a colleague. It is called konsolofon, as it is made with shelf brackets ("konsoler" in Swedish) and is based on the xylophone, but is similar in frequency to bells. The shelf brackets was cut with a notch with a specific length to achieve the aimed frequency and tone of each shelf bracket. The konsolofon is tuned in E minor blues scale.

Dark Exploration

In this project, an exploration through sound was developed in the course Sound in interaction at KTH. The user get to explore a room in darkness through sound. Every wall is mapped with an instrument, from a music track consisting of four instruments. In the middle of the room, the sweet spot is detected, but when moving toward a specific wall, the specific mapped instrument will be heard more and the user can in turn get help through sound to locate in the room.

This project was developed together with a colleague in SuperCollider and used Motion Capture to detect the movements.

MixAR

MixAR is a mobile application where the user can go between musicians in an ensemble and listen to the specific instrument played. It was developed in the course Multimodal Interactions and Interfaces at KTH. The instruments, or musician, can be placed differently using cards. This can make the user listen to an ensemble in a bigger or smaller room and listen to how the spatial sound of the ensemble will be like.

It was developed in Unity using Augmented Reality (AR)

Christmas Hero

In a Computer game course taken in 2019, a Christmas themed game was developed by me and a team of four others. In the game, the spirit of Christmas had disappeard but was progressively coming back when Christmas gifts were delivered to the right character. The music was therefore progressively becoming more christmassy in terms of Christmas related instruments and melody. In level 1, only wind sound was heard. In level 2, I wanted to make a simple and quite careful melody that felt hopeful and happy but not too christmassy. As I wanted a feeling of continuation between the levels, the beginning of the music in level 3 has the same melody as in level 2, but is then continuing in melody. In the final part, I wanted to create a happy and as christmassy feeling as possible. As the user has reached the end of the game by then, it is relativly short and is not repeating.

The composition was made in MuseScore by me. What was challenging was that it was hard knowing how long a level will take to play, as the composition was made while the game was developed. That made it hard to know how long a piece should be and how many musical features to be made. I made the level pieces therefore quite short that could be repeated.
